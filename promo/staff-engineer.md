# Promotion Criteria: Senior Software Engineer → Staff Engineer

## Overview
This document outlines the expectations for promotion from Senior Software Engineer (L5) to Staff Engineer (L6). Staff engineers are expected to demonstrate sustained impact beyond their immediate team, technical leadership across multiple systems, and the ability to drive ambiguous, high-stakes projects to completion.

Promotion requires evidence across all four dimensions below. Isolated examples are not sufficient — the candidate must show a consistent pattern over 12–18 months.

---

## Dimension 1: Technical Impact & Scope

**Expectation:** The candidate delivers technical solutions with meaningful, measurable impact on the product, platform, or organization — not just within a single team or service.

- Owns and ships projects of high complexity and ambiguity (no well-defined spec at the start)
- Technical decisions last: designs are durable, not quickly replaced or worked around
- Demonstrates sound architectural judgment — trade-offs are understood and documented
- Work has measurable outcomes: performance improvements, reliability gains, cost reduction, user impact

**Evidence to provide:**
- List 2–3 projects that best demonstrate technical scope and impact
- For each: what was ambiguous at the start, what decisions were made, what was the result

---

## Dimension 2: Engineering Excellence & Craft

**Expectation:** The candidate consistently raises the technical bar for the team. Code, systems, and processes they touch are better than when they found them.

- Champions testing, observability, and operational readiness — not just as personal practice but as team norms
- Identifies and reduces systemic technical debt with clear business justification
- Actively contributes to code review culture, setting standards rather than just following them
- Demonstrates mastery of the relevant technical stack and makes informed choices between alternatives

**Evidence to provide:**
- Examples of raising team-wide practices (code review standards, testing culture, incident process)
- A specific case where engineering craft directly prevented or resolved a production issue

---

## Dimension 3: Leadership & Influence

**Expectation:** The candidate multiplies the effectiveness of those around them. Staff engineers lead through influence, not authority.

- Leads multi-engineer projects end-to-end: planning, execution, unblocking, delivery
- Actively develops junior and mid-level engineers through pairing, code review, and design feedback
- Builds alignment across teams — navigates competing priorities and organizational friction
- Recognized by peers and partner teams as a go-to person for technical judgment

**Evidence to provide:**
- A project where you led a team: how did you set direction, handle disagreement, and keep things moving?
- Examples of engineers you've grown or unblocked, with specific outcomes

---

## Dimension 4: Organizational Contribution

**Expectation:** The candidate invests in the health and effectiveness of the engineering organization, not just their direct work.

- Improves processes, tooling, or practices in ways that outlast their individual involvement
- Contributes to recruiting, onboarding, or engineering culture initiatives
- Communicates technical decisions clearly to non-technical stakeholders
- Demonstrates ownership: takes responsibility for problems even outside their nominal scope

**Evidence to provide:**
- Examples of organizational investments: what problem existed, what you did, what changed
- Evidence that the improvement persisted and was adopted broadly

---

## Promo Packet Instructions

The promotion document should be structured as follows:

1. **Summary** — A 2–3 sentence statement of why this candidate is ready for Staff. Written as if the candidate's manager is presenting to the promotion committee.

2. **Project Highlights** — For each of 2–3 key projects, a short narrative (4–6 sentences) covering: the challenge, the candidate's role, the technical decisions made, and the measurable outcome.

3. **Leadership & Mentorship** — Specific examples of how the candidate has grown others and led across team boundaries.

4. **Engineering Excellence** — Concrete examples of how the candidate has raised the technical bar for the team.

5. **Organizational Contributions** — What has the candidate improved that goes beyond their direct project work?

6. **Peer Quotes** (optional placeholder) — 1–2 sample quotes the candidate could solicit from colleagues that would strengthen the case.

---

*Leveling calibration reference: Staff Engineers are expected to operate at the scope of a team or cross-team initiative. They do not need to be managers, but they are force multipliers. The question to ask is: would the team meaningfully slow down without this person? Would their absence leave a gap that could not be filled by promoting an existing senior?*
