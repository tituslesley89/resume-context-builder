# Promotion Criteria: Staff Engineer → Principal Engineer

## Overview
This document outlines the expectations for promotion from Staff Engineer (L6) to Principal Engineer (L7). Principal engineers operate at the scope of an organization or business unit — not a team or a set of teams. Their work shapes multi-year technical direction and has compounding, lasting impact on the company's ability to build and ship.

This is a rare bar. Most engineers who can clear it are already doing significant portions of the Principal role before the title is conferred. If the evidence below requires stretching or inference, the candidate is not yet ready.

---

## Dimension 1: Organizational Technical Strategy

**Expectation:** The candidate shapes the technical direction of a significant part of the organization — not just a team or product area — and that direction is adopted and sustained.

- Has defined or substantially influenced a multi-year technical roadmap that is followed by multiple teams
- Identifies structural problems (architectural debt, platform gaps, systemic reliability issues) before they become crises, and drives resolution
- Technical decisions they've made are visible across the organization and referenced by other engineers in their own work
- Has made calls that involved significant trade-offs with long-term implications, and those calls have held up

**Evidence to provide:**
- 1–2 technical strategies or architectural decisions made at organizational scope: what the problem was, what alternatives were considered, what was decided, and what has happened since
- Evidence that others are building on or referencing this work

---

## Dimension 2: Cross-Organizational Influence & Alignment

**Expectation:** The candidate drives alignment across teams, orgs, and sometimes the industry — not through authority, but through credibility, communication, and persistence.

- Regularly operates across team and org boundaries to solve problems that no single team owns
- Has navigated and resolved significant technical disagreements between senior engineers or teams — not by pulling rank, but by building shared understanding
- Is sought out by senior leaders (VPs, directors, other principals) for technical counsel on high-stakes decisions
- Has influenced engineering culture or practice at company scope, not just their own team

**Evidence to provide:**
- A cross-org problem the candidate drove to resolution: what was the disagreement or gap, who was involved, and how did they build alignment?
- Examples of being sought out by senior leadership for technical input

---

## Dimension 3: External Impact & Technical Reputation

**Expectation:** The candidate's influence extends beyond the company — through open source, publications, conference talks, standards work, or industry recognition. This is not a hard requirement at all companies, but it is a strong signal.

- Has made meaningful contributions to open source projects, technical standards, or industry bodies in their domain
- Has written or spoken publicly about technical topics in a way that has reached and influenced the field
- Is recognized by name outside the company as a credible voice in their area of expertise
- Attracts strong engineering talent to the company through their public presence

**Evidence to provide:**
- Specific external contributions with evidence of reach or impact
- Examples of candidates or partners who cited the engineer's public work

---

## Dimension 4: Developing Other Senior Engineers

**Expectation:** The candidate multiplies the effectiveness of Staff and Senior engineers, not just juniors. They raise the ceiling for the people already operating at a high level.

- Sponsors and develops Staff engineers: helps them find organizational leverage, get visibility, and scope their own impact
- Runs or shapes technical forums, design reviews, or other structures that elevate technical decision-making across the org
- Has directly contributed to the promotions of Staff-level engineers through investment and advocacy
- Creates engineering culture that outlasts their individual involvement

**Evidence to provide:**
- Staff engineers the candidate has invested in, with specific examples of what changed for them
- Systems or practices the candidate put in place that operate independently of their ongoing involvement

---

## Dimension 5: Judgment Under Ambiguity at Scale

**Expectation:** The candidate makes high-stakes technical decisions when there is no clear right answer — and they do it in a way that brings others along, not just reaches an answer.

- Has navigated large-scale migrations, platform changes, or architectural transitions with significant business risk
- Makes decisions that require weighing technical, product, organizational, and financial trade-offs simultaneously
- Knows when to move decisively and when to slow down and build consensus — and is right more often than not
- When they are wrong, they correct course quickly and constructively

**Evidence to provide:**
- A high-stakes decision made under genuine uncertainty: what was the risk, what was the decision, and how did it turn out?
- An example of changing course — what led to it and how was it handled?

---

## Promo Packet Instructions

The promotion document should be structured as follows:

1. **Summary** — 2–3 sentences on why this candidate is operating at Principal scope. Should convey organizational impact, not just technical excellence.

2. **Technical Strategy & Organizational Impact** — 1–2 narratives showing the candidate's work shaping multi-team or multi-year direction, with evidence of lasting impact.

3. **Cross-Organizational Influence** — Specific examples of driving alignment at scale, including senior stakeholders involved and how disagreement was navigated.

4. **Developing Senior Engineers** — How the candidate has grown Staff-level engineers and built structures that elevate the organization.

5. **External Presence** (if applicable) — Contributions to the field outside the company.

6. **Judgment at Scale** — High-stakes decisions made under ambiguity, including at least one case where the candidate course-corrected.

---

*Leveling calibration reference: The Staff bar is "does the team slow down without this person?" The Principal bar is higher: "does the engineering organization's strategic capability meaningfully diminish without this person?" If the answer is "we'd be fine in six months after backfilling," that's a Staff engineer. A Principal is someone whose absence would create a gap in organizational judgment that is very hard to fill.*
